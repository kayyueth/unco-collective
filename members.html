Profile · F·001

Kay Yu · Fellow, Lead Author

Kay Yu

Uncommons Core · Hong Kong & Remote

Kay is a founding fellow of Uncommons Collective and served as primary author of the Paper-as-Method proposal. Her research focuses on governance institutions in the Chinese-speaking crypto ecosystem, translation as a form of political work, and the conditions under which decentralized organizations produce durable institutions rather than ephemeral communities.

She has hosted the Uncommons salon series since 2023 and maintains an annotated reading list of blockchain governance scholarship used across several reading groups. Her current paper — UNC·2026·01 — asks who owns the compute substrate of the next internet.

Current sprint · UNC·2026·01 (lead)
Contributing to · UNC·2026·03, UNC·2026·05
Languages · English, Mandarin, Cantonese

Profile · F·002

Twone · Fellow, Lead Author

Twone

Independent Protocol Researcher · Taipei

Twone is a founding fellow and the collective's editor for technical papers. His background combines protocol engineering and political economy — an unusual pairing that shaped the translation tradition on which Uncommons was built. He has translated five canonical texts on governance into Chinese, including Hirschman's Exit, Voice, and Loyalty, which remains the most-read piece in the Uncommons archive.

His current paper — UNC·2026·03 — re-examines the claim that mechanism design can substitute for institutional trust, through four empirical case studies in DAO governance. He maintains the collective's editorial standards document.

Current sprint · UNC·2026·03 (lead)
Contributing to · UNC·2026·01, UNC·2026·04
Editorial lead · Technical papers

Profile · F·003

Big Song · Fellow, Lead Author

Big Song

Protocol Research · Singapore

Big Song brings the collective's quantitative muscle. A former quantitative researcher at a market-making firm, he pivoted to onchain data in 2022 and has since become one of the more reliable voices on MEV, order-flow, and market-structure questions that most writers prefer to handwave. His contribution is a willingness to let the data lead.

His current paper — UNC·2026·04 — proposes a taxonomy of MEV as a precondition for coherent regulation. He also maintains an open dataset of cross-chain MEV extraction used by several other researchers in the field.

Current sprint · UNC·2026·04 (lead)
Contributing to · UNC·2026·02, UNC·2026·03, UNC·2026·06
Data · Public onchain analytics

Profile · F·004

Fang Ting · Fellow, Lead Author

Fang Ting

Academia · Law & Technology · Beijing / Remote

Fang Ting is the collective's legal-theoretic anchor. Her doctoral work on regulatory theory and her subsequent policy briefings on AI governance make her uniquely placed to write on questions at the boundary of law, technology, and political economy. She is especially attentive to the ways in which governance language imported from crypto fails to translate into jurisdictional reality.

Her current paper — UNC·2026·05 — asks whether autonomous agents should hold a limited, revocable form of legal personhood. She contributes the legal framework chapter to UNC·2026·01 and is the collective's primary liaison with policy institutes.

Current sprint · UNC·2026·05 (proposed lead)
Contributing to · UNC·2026·01, UNC·2026·03, UNC·2026·06
External · Policy institute advisory

Profile · F·005

Shirlene · Fellow, Lead Author

Shirlene

Academia · Digital Sociology · Shanghai

Shirlene is the collective's sociologist of the commons. Her work traces how reputation, credentials, and trust move between online and offline institutions — and what breaks when those migrations are taken for granted. She is skeptical of the enthusiasm with which technical communities import "governance," and her writing is the closest thing the collective has to an institutional conscience.

She leads two papers: UNC·2026·02 on onchain reputation as an institutional primitive, and UNC·2026·06 on the retroactive public- goods funding experiments. She also runs the collective's ongoing reading group on commons theory.

Current sprint · UNC·2026·02 (lead), UNC·2026·06 (lead)
Contributing to · UNC·2026·05
Program · Commons reading group
