About
the Collective.

Uncommons is a networked research collective for producing public-facing thought and applied research on emerging internet institutions. Founded April 2026 in Hong Kong. Remote-first. English and Chinese working languages.

§ 01 · History

From salon to collective.

Uncommons began in 2023 as a loose reading group in Hong Kong and Taipei — a handful of researchers, translators, and practitioners who found mainstream crypto discourse thin on institutional thinking and the humanities thin on the technical reality. For two years we ran salons, translated canonical texts, and hosted long-form interviews with figures across the Chinese- speaking ecosystem.

By late 2025 the activity had generated real cultural value but suffered from three structural weaknesses: no narrative thesis, no minimum viable unit of work, no output flywheel. We operated like a salon but wanted to act like an institution.

In April 2026 the founding fellows ratified the Paper-as-Method proposal and restructured the community as a research collective. The salon continues. But the center of gravity is now the paper.

§ 02 · Governance

Lightweight by design.

Uncommons operates under a lightweight editorial governance model. The collective is not a DAO and does not aspire to be one. We run on the minimum bureaucracy required to produce and publish rigorous research.

Thesis ratification. Any member may propose a thesis. A thesis is ratified when 4–5 Research Members commit to forming a cell around it. The editorial board retains a final veto only on grounds of fit, not quality — quality is decided by the peer-review stage.

Editorial board. Three fellows, rotating annually. Responsible for final publication approval, editorial standards, and dispute resolution. Currently: Kay Yu (chair), Twone, Shirlene.

Conflict and exit. Every cell publishes a roles document at Week 2. Authorship order is determined by contribution and agreed before Week 5. Members may exit a cell at any time; the cell decides whether and how to continue without them.

Editorial independence. Sponsored research carries a non-negotiable editorial independence clause. Funders may propose topics. Funders do not shape conclusions.

§ 04 · Financial Architecture

Research as
intellectual property.

Uncommons is not a charity. Revenue is diversified across four channels, ordered by readiness. All sponsored research carries an editorial independence clause.

Active now

Donations & Patronage

Individual and institutional supporters fund the collective through Mirror, Substack, or direct donation. Research outputs provide clear justification: patrons fund specific papers with visible authorship and public impact.

Near-term

Sponsored Research

Protocols, foundations, and think tanks commission Uncommons to produce research on specific topics. Funders propose topics but do not control conclusions. Editorial independence is non-negotiable.

Medium-term

Advisory & Consulting

Governance design reviews for DAOs, policy briefings for regulators, and strategic research for foundations. Priced at institutional rates and distributed across participating members.

Long-term

Education Products

Workshops, cohort-based courses, and reading curricula built from research outputs. Target: first education product after 4–6 completed research cycles.
