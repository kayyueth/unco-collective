Overview

Bitcoin = Ideas + Technology + Practice. This column reconstructs its pre-history as a group portrait — not a hagiography, not a whitepaper archaeology, but documentary non-fiction that treats its subjects as human beings shaped by their moment.

The analytical lens is historical materialism: technology does not emerge from genius alone. The Crypto Wars, the collapse of Bretton Woods, the 2008 financial meltdown — these are not background context. They are the forces that made the cryptographers' work legible, urgent, and eventually inevitable.

Column Structure
  • I Big History (5–10k) — The political-economic conditions that made Bitcoin necessary: Cold War cryptography, the Crypto Wars, financial crises, the surveillance state.
  • II Group Portraits (5k × 6) — Six figures written together: how their contributions intersected, where they disagreed, what each one added to the chain that became Bitcoin.
  • III Opinion (5–10k) — Two analytical threads: Technology × Big History (Kay) and Technology × Justice (Shiyu).
Analytical Perspectives
Thread 01 · Kay Yu

Technology × Big History

How do macro political-economic conditions shape which technologies get invented, by whom, and why they succeed or fail? The cryptographers' story is inseparable from the surveillance apparatus, the collapse of analog monetary sovereignty, and the ideological vacuum left by the Cold War's end.

Thread 02 · Shiyu

Technology × Justice

A technology built on libertarian premises — radical individual sovereignty, trustlessness, the elimination of intermediaries — became a dark jungle amplifying human vice. What happens when tools of freedom become instruments of capture?
