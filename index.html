Mission
A networked research collective for producing public-facing thought and applied research on emerging internet institutions.

Uncommons began with translation, reading groups, and salons at the intersection of blockchain and the humanities. We restructure around a single organizing insight: the co-authored research paper is the optimal minimum viable unit of collective intellectual work.

We are not a passive mailing list, a protocol think-tank, or a media outlet. We are a production environment for rigorous, public-interest research — built to run at the pace of ideas, not conferences.

AI Sovereignty and the Compute Layer: Who Owns the Substrate of the Next Internet?

Kay Yu (lead) · Fang Ting · Twone · Shirlene · Big Song

AI Policy Sovereignty Infrastructure Geopolitics

The debate over "AI sovereignty" has largely focused on model access and training data. We argue this framing misses the more durable political question: who owns the compute substrate on which foundation models run? This paper maps the geopolitics of the GPU supply chain, the data-center buildout, and the emerging sovereign-compute initiatives from Europe to the Gulf, and asks what meaningful sovereignty looks like when the stack is global but the silicon is not.

Why Papers

The paper is not the output.
It is the method.

A paper demands a thesis, assembles a team, runs a process, and generates a cascade of derivative outputs. It is the organizational primitive from which everything else derives.

01

Thesis-driven

Forces the collective to take a position. Each sprint begins with a proposition, not a topic.

02

Team-forming

Each paper assembles a working group with clear roles: lead author, contributors, editor.

03

Time-boxed

A 4–6 week sprint creates urgency and prevents drift. The timeline is the discipline.

04

Compounding

Each paper generates 4–5 derivative outputs — threads, podcasts, salons — that seed the next thesis.
